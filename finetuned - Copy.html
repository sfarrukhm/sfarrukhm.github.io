Fine-Tuning Projects

Fine-Tuning DistilBERT for Medical Reasoning with Domain Adaptation

In this project, I fine-tuned a DistilBERT model (distilbert-base-uncased) on the medical-o1-reasoning-SFT dataset to enhance its reasoning capabilities in the medical domain.

What I have learned:

  • Fine-tuning a model through Masked Language Modeling for domain adaptation.
  • Understanding and computing cross-entropy and perplexity for language modeling tasks.
  • Concept and benefits of mixed precision training (FP16 vs. FP32).

Fine-Tuning BERT-based Model for Named Entity Recognition (NER)

Fine-tuned a google-bert model for Named Entity Recognition (NER) using the CoNLL-2003 dataset to classify entities like names, organizations, and locations.

What I have learned:

  • Understanding the concept of token classification and its distinction from sequence classification.
  • Learning the purpose and functionality of the DataCollatorForTokenClassification module of 🤗 transformers.
  • Handling token alignment and adjusting labels during subword tokenization.

Fine-Tuning ModernBERT for Natural Language Inference (NLI)

Fine-tuned the ModernBERT-base model on a subset of the SetFit/mnli dataset for Natural Language Inference (NLI), aiming to determine relationships between text inputs (e.g., entailment, contradiction, or neutrality).

What I have learned:

  • Through this project, I learned about Natural Language Inference (NLI) and how to prepare data for fine-tuning a model for NLI tasks.
